Untitled
A fragile chain. Or so I thought of it, in the way you think about things so they fit into the existing contours of your brain - Neat and without blistering the mind's edges. A fragile chain, coursing through women who duck their shoulders, Who sketch worlds in the veil of a shadow and toss them in the same movement. And it aches the way I feel the maternal shrink and liquify into a small upstream to infect my own circuitry, And the way I feel a man with buckles on his shoes take giant steps forward out of my chest and into the room. But as the years pass, I learn the chain is not just fragile, not just breaking in all of its length and width - It is not. You can take your hands and grab it and feel it, and you can pull all the way through time, kneeding its strength and weight and girth, then feel it as it whittles to a string that cuts into your skin. It is a chain with decisions to make. It is a chain under siege. It is a chain like an axon. And it travels all the way to a piece of land with olives and lemons. And a graphite pencil and sunlight hitting the house cat just right, and a dream.
